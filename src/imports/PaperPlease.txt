?? Design Review & Recommendations (from the Game Design Team's POV)
?? 1. Onboarding

Problem: The game's recursion and lore depth could fry new players' brains immediately.
Fix:

Start with a "fake tutorial" that pretends to be a normal OS setup wizard.
As it runs, small anomalies appear-fonts drift, options rewrite themselves-teaching the player the game's core verbs (open, type, connect) without saying it's a game.

Use progressive revelation: early puzzles mimic normal desktop functions (open files, rearrange icons). Each successful action secretly unlocks lore without overwhelming exposition.

Optional "Memory Log" menu lets players rewatch discovered lore fragments in chronological order.

?? 2. Technical Feasibility

Problem: You're blending reality bleed, ARG integration, and fake OS simulation. That's powerful but risky.
Fix:

Sandbox everything in a self-contained "virtual desktop" window. Never touch actual file systems.

Simulate real-world file behavior (copy, delete, open) using mirrored fake directories.

Reality bleed effects-like fake pop-ups, "desktop corruption," or camera reflection tricks-should only occur within the safe sandbox UI.

For ARG elements, host them on a dedicated site or Discord bot to avoid social platform chaos.

?? 3. Puzzle Design

Problem: Recursive mechanics can easily cause soft-locks or impossible states.
Fix:

Every major puzzle should have two layers: the "surface" (the literal puzzle) and the "reflection" (the meta meaning). Both should be solvable independently so players never brick progress.

Introduce a "System Restore" mechanic-lore-justified checkpoints ("Reboot from Version Snapshot"). It's diegetic, not immersion-breaking.

Have the OS "help" subtly by pretending to glitch in hints ("error: maybe check mirror logs again?").

?? 4. Community Management

Problem: ARG and community submissions = chaos if left unchecked.
Fix:

Keep ARG participation opt-in. Players must "connect to the external network" manually.

Implement a moderation queue for community puzzle submissions (auto-flag NSFW or unsafe content).

In-lore justification: "The Lake filters reflections that threaten stability."

Include an ARG safety disclaimer disguised as a system warning (fits the vibe and the ethics).

?? 5. Narrative Consistency

Problem: Your story folds in on itself like origami made of wet dreams. Maintaining coherence will be brutal.
Fix:

Build a timeline spreadsheet that tracks each entity (Architect, Mirror Child, User, The Lake) and how they appear in every phase. Color-code their "truth states" (real, reflection, corrupted).

Appoint a "Lore Custodian" (even if it's just you with a fancy title). Their job is to sanity-check all new content for timeline consistency.
